troff

A program for formatting documents in the Unix document processing system

Troff is the typesetter of Unix’s document-processing pipeline, written at Bell Labs in the 1970s. It stands for “typesetter roff” and descends from roff, where roff was a Unix version of one of the earliest text formatters, RUNOFF. A typical troff distribution ships with macro packages for common document styles, including the one used for Unix man pages.
